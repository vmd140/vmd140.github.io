Biography

American artist Kiki Smith (Born Nuremberg, Germany, Jan 18, 1954) is an installation artist who has worked in a variety of mediums including bronze casting, glass blowing, ceramics, lithography and other print processes, encaustic painting, and environmental design. Her work frequently deals with themes of mortality, the human form, and its various bodily functions.

After a brief stint at Hartford Art School in Connecticut, Smith moved to New York city and joined COLAB (Collaborative Projects) a radical artist collective in 1976. Their use of unconventional materials heavily influenced Smith's continued exploration of a wide variety of mediums. She also briefly studied to be an EMT, which prompted her first sculptures of body parts-- a recurring theme in her work. Her ongoing fascination with mortality stems in part from the death of her sculptor father in 1980, and the death of her sister, underground film actor Beatrice (Bebe) Smith, of AIDS, in 1988.

Notable Works

Some of her most notable works include:

  • "Mary Magdelene" (1994), a bronze and steel sculpture of a female nude
  • "Homespun Tales" (2005), an installation at the 51st Venice Biennele
  • "Sojourn" (2010), an installation at Brooklyn Museum
