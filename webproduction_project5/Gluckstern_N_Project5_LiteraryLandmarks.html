LITERARY LANDMARKS

City Lights Books 261 Columbus St.
The nation's first all-paperback bookstore, City Lights was founded in 1953 by Lawrence Ferlinghetti and Peter D. Martin. They've been publshing books since 1955, and were put on trial for obscenity in 1956 for publishing the seminal Beat poem, "Howl," by Allen Ginsberg.

Beat Museum 540 Broadway
Through the museum’s quirky collection visitors learn about the cold war context of the Beat Generation’s emergence, and their influence on the counterculture of the 1960s.

RE/Search 20 Romolo
RE/Search was first published in 1970 as a punk fanzine, Search and Destroy , by V. Vale. Changed name and format in 1980, and expanded the focus to documenting subcultures and iconoclasts of many kinds.
