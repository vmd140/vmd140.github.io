LITERARY BARS AND CAFES

Caffe Trieste 609 Vallejo
Although Cafe Trieste has the backstory and “must visit” status of a tourist trap, it never feels like one. At its core, Cafe Trieste is the place where North Beach residents have been gathering since 1956, to mingle, to write, to catch up on the gossip, to listen to Saturday afternoon opera, and to caffeinate.

Vesuvio 255 Columbus
Henri Lenoir opened Vesuvio in 1948. His vision was to create “a bohemian meeting place for artists to come to life.” Vesuvio has been the center of North Beach social life ever since.

Gino and Carlo 548 Green
One of North Beach’s last remaining “old school” Italian bars, Gino and Carlo's was considered the “living room” of 1950’s poet, linguist, and lecturer Jack Spicer, a co-founder of the infamous Six Gallery, where Allen Ginsberg debuted “Howl."
